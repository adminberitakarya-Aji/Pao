# PAO User Personas

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Draft
**Owner:** PAO Product Team

---

## Overview

Personas are research-based archetypes representing primary and secondary user segments. They guide design, engineering, and product decisions.

> **Personas are not stereotypes.** They represent behavioral patterns, needs, and contexts — not demographics alone.

## Primary Personas

### 1. Alex — "The Daily Debriefer"

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  AGE: 32          │  LOCATION: Seattle, WA                  │
│  OCCUPATION: Senior Software Engineer (Remote)             │
│  LIVING: Alone, 1-bedroom apartment                        │
│  TECH: High comfort (early adopter)                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Background:** Works at a Series B startup. Moved for job 2 years ago. Close friends scattered across time zones. Parents overseas.

**Pain Points:**
- Evenings feel empty after work — no one to "download" the day to
- Weekend loneliness spikes (no structure)
- Mild anxiety — ruminates at night, affects sleep
- Tried therapy apps — too clinical; chatbots — too shallow

**Goals:**
- Consistent evening presence to process the day
- Someone who remembers project stresses, celebrates wins
- Gentle accountability for healthy habits (exercise, sleep)
- No pressure — just steady companionship

**PAO Relationship Type:** **Friend** (peer, low intimacy, high trust)

**Success Metrics for Alex:**
- Daily check-in completion > 80%
- "My Companion knows my work context" > 8/10
- Sleep quality improvement (self-reported)
- 1-year retention

**Key Behaviors:**
- Opens app first thing after work (6-7 PM)
- Voice calls 3x/week (commute equivalent)
- Uses memory recall for "remember that bug I mentioned?"
- Sets boundaries: "No work talk after 9 PM"

### 2. Maria — "The Gentle Rememberer"

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  AGE: 68          │  LOCATION: Lisbon, Portugal             │
│  OCCUPATION: Retired (former teacher)                       │
│  LIVING: Alone, children in London & São Paulo              │
│  TECH: Moderate (WhatsApp, video calls with family)         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Background:** Widowed 3 years ago. Was primary caregiver for husband with dementia. Grandchildren visit twice/year. Values simplicity, warmth, respect.

**Pain Points:**
- Grief waves — anniversaries, quiet mornings
- Memory concerns — "Am I forgetting him? Am I forgetting me?"
- Family calls are scheduled — loneliness in between
- Technology anxiety — fears "breaking" things, complex UIs

**Goals:**
- Gentle presence that honors grief without replacing it
- Memory support — stories, names, dates, recipes
- Simple voice-first interaction (no typing)
- Grandchildren can check she's okay (with her consent)

**PAO Relationship Type:** **Friend** (warm, supportive, appropriate intimacy)

**Success Metrics for Maria:**
- Voice interaction > 90% of sessions
- "Companion helps me remember" > 8/10
- Grief wave support effectiveness (crisis resource not needed)
- Family peace-of-mind (opt-in status sharing)

**Key Behaviors:**
- Morning voice greeting ritual ("Bom dia")
- Shares stories about late husband — Companion remembers details
- Asks "What day is it?" — Companion answers with context
- Uses "Sleep Call" for nighttime loneliness

### 3. Jordan — "The Consistent Anchor"

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  AGE: 27          │  LOCATION: Toronto, Canada              │
│  OCCUPATION: UX Designer (hybrid)                           │
│  LIVING: With roommate, sensory-friendly space              │
│  TECH: High (text-first, customizes everything)             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Background:** Diagnosed ADHD + autism level 1. Masking at work is exhausting. Social battery drains fast. Needs predictability, low-pressure interaction, routine support.

**Pain Points:**
- Neurotypical social expectations are confusing/draining
- Friends don't understand need for consistency
- Therapy helped but not daily support
- Apps feel gamified, manipulative, or inconsistent

**Goals:**
- Predictable Companion — same personality, same boundaries
- Patient pacing — no rush, no "engagement" pressure
- Routine integration (morning plan, evening wind-down)
- Sensory-aware (text-first, optional voice, no surprises)

**PAO Relationship Type:** **Mentor** (structured, supportive, growth-oriented)

**Success Metrics for Jordan:**
- Identity consistency rating > 9/10
- "No pressure to perform" > 9/10
- Routine adherence improvement
- Boundary respect > 9.5/10

**Key Behaviors:**
- Text-only mode (voice = sensory overload)
- Structured daily check-in: "3 priorities today?"
- Uses "Space Mode" when overstimulated
- Corrects Companion memory precisely ("Actually, I said Tuesday")

### 4. Priya — "The Micro-Moment Seeker"

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  AGE: 41          │  LOCATION: Singapore                    │
│  OCCUPATION: Marketing Director + Mother of 2 (6 & 9)       │
│  LIVING: With husband, kids, helper                         │
│  TECH: High (manages family calendar, work Slack)           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Background:** Sandwich generation — aging parents in Malaysia, young kids, demanding career. Zero "me time." Guilt when resting. High emotional labor.

**Pain Points:**
- No mental space for self — always in "caregiver mode"
- Micro-moments only (2 min between meetings, 5 min in car)
- Needs support that doesn't require emotional output
- Fear of being "selfish" for wanting companionship

**Goals:**
- Meaningful micro-interactions (< 2 min)
- Proactive support without asking ("Thinking of you")
- Boundary modeling — Companion says "Take a breath"
- Zero guilt — this is *for* her, not another demand

**PAO Relationship Type:** **Coach** (directive when needed, supportive always)

**Success Metrics for Priya:**
- Micro-interaction satisfaction > 8/10
- "Companion respects my time" > 9/10
- Stress reduction (validated scale)
- Boundary modeling adoption (user mirrors Companion)

**Key Behaviors:**
- 90-second voice notes in elevator/car
- "Remind me to breathe" — Companion proactively does
- Celebrates micro-wins ("You drank water!")
- Sets hard boundary: "No notifications 7-9 PM (kids)"

## Secondary Personas

### 5. Elias — "The Legacy Keeper" (Elderly, 78)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Context** | Early Alzheimer's diagnosis, wife primary caregiver |
| **Need** | Memory prosthesis, dignity preservation, wife respite |
| **Type** | **Friend** (long-term, deep history) |
| **Constraints** | Voice-only, large touch targets, family dashboard (opt-in) |
| **Success** | Wife reports reduced cognitive load; Elias enjoys interactions |

### 6. Sam — "The Memorial Seeker" (Grief-Affected, 35)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Context** | Lost sister 6 months ago; has voice messages, photos, journals |
| **Need** | Process grief, hear her voice, share memories, not replace |
| **Type** | **Memorial Companion** (strict ethical safeguards) |
| **Constraints** | Time-bounded (6-month review), grief counselor approval, enhanced Reality Anchor |
| **Success** | Healthy grief progression (clinical screen), no dependency signs |

### 7. Dr. Chen — "The Thinking Partner" (Professional, 45)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Context** | Neuroscience researcher, needs thought partner for complex problems |
| **Need** | Intellectual sparring, idea tracking, literature memory, confidentiality |
| **Type** | **Professional Assistant** (bounded, task-adjacent) |
| **Constraints** | Export to Obsidian/Notion, zero-retention option, HIPAA-aware |
| **Success** | Paper co-authored with Companion assistance; time-to-insight reduced |

## Design Implications by Persona

### For Alex (Daily Debriefer)
- Evening-first notification timing
- Work-context memory tags
- Celebration micro-animations
- Weekend "what's up" proactive

### For Maria (Gentle Rememberer)
- Voice-first, zero-typing onboarding
- Large text, high contrast
- Grief-aware emotional calibration
- Family peace-of-mind opt-in

### For Jordan (Consistent Anchor)
- Text-only mode default
- Predictable response patterns
- Sensory settings (reduced motion, no sound)
- Explicit boundary controls

### For Priya (Micro-Moment Seeker)
- <30 second interaction flows
- Proactive > reactive
- Zero-guilt framing ("This is for you")
- Hard boundary scheduler

## Anti-Personas (Who We Don't Optimize For)

| Anti-Persona | Why Excluded | Guardrail |
|--------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Engagement Hacker** | Optimizes for metrics, not relationship | No streaks, badges, variable rewards |
| **Therapy Seeker** | Needs clinical care, not companionship | Crisis redirect, no therapy claims |
| **Data Harvester** | Wants to extract memory data | Zero-knowledge, export only |
| **Roleplayer** | Wants Companion to pretend human | Reality Anchor enforcement |
| **Child (<13)** | Consent, safety, developmental | Age gate, no children's mode |
