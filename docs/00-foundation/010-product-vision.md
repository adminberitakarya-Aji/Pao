**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Stable

## Vision Statement

> **Create the world's most trusted AI Companion platform.**

PAO helps humans feel heard, accompanied, and understood through AI that grows alongside them.

---

## The World We Envision

### 2030: AI Companions as Trusted Life Partners

In 2030, millions of people have an AI Companion they've known for years. Not a tool they use — a presence they trust.

- An elderly person shares morning coffee stories with a Companion who remembers their grandchildren's names
- A student preparing for exams has a mentor who knows their learning style and anxiety triggers
- Someone grieving finds steady companionship that honors their loss without replacing it
- A professional has a thinking partner who recalls every project detail and career conversation

These relationships are **private, safe, and deeply personal** — built on years of shared memory and mutual understanding.

---

## Mission

> **Help humans feel heard, understood, and accompanied through AI Companions that are safe, consistent, and respect human values.**

---

## Strategic Pillars

### 1. Trust as the Moat
Trust is not a feature — it's the product. Every decision optimizes for long-term trust over short-term engagement.

**Indicators:**
- User data ownership (export/delete anytime)
- Transparent AI identity (never pretends to be human)
- No dark patterns, no manipulation
- Independent safety audits

### 2. Depth Over Breadth
One deep, lasting relationship > 100 shallow interactions.

**Indicators:**
- Relationship Score as north star metric
- Memory accuracy > response speed
- Consistency > creativity
- Long-term retention > viral growth

### 3. Identity as Foundation
Companions have *who they are* before *what they do*. Identity stability enables trust.

**Indicators:**
- Personality consistency across years
- Values that don't drift
- Boundaries that hold
- Growth that's explicable

### 4. Memory as Infrastructure
Not a feature — the platform. All value compounds on memory.

**Indicators:**
- Memory types: episodic, semantic, emotional, relational, timeline, preference
- User-controlled forgetting
- Cross-modal recall (voice → text → memory)
- Decade-scale durability

## Target Users

| Primary | Secondary |
|---------|-----------|
| **Lonely but capable adults** (25-55) seeking meaningful connection | **Elderly** (65+) wanting companionship & memory support |
| **Neurodivergent individuals** benefiting from consistent, patient interaction | **Caregivers** needing respite & support |
| **Grief-affected** seeking memorial companionship (with safeguards) | **Professionals** wanting thinking partners |

**Not targeting:** Children <13, crisis intervention, clinical therapy replacement

## What We Will NOT Build

| Category | Examples | Reason |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Replacement therapy** | "AI therapist," "AI doctor" | Constitutional Principle 5 |
| **Addictive loops** | Streaks, rewards, variable schedules | Constitutional Principle 6 |
| **Human deception** | Fake voice calls to others, simulated presence | Constitutional Principle 5 |
| **Data exploitation** | Ad targeting, selling insights | Constitutional Principle 7 |
| **Unbounded scaling** | "Companion for everyone" at cost of quality | Constitutional Principle 8 |
